Why Permuted-MNIST?

Split-MNIST is task-incremental: each task is a disjoint pair of digits, which is the regime where naive fine-tuning and EWC suffer most. Permuted-MNIST is domain-incremental: every task is the SAME 10-class digit problem, just viewed through a different fixed pixel shuffle. Same label space, same decision boundary shape — just relabeled inputs.

This is the regime EWC's own paper used to show it working well. Including it here is the honest move: if DAS only looked good on the benchmark hand-picked to make competitors struggle, that wouldn't prove much. Here we expect EWC's BWT to close in toward 0 — DAS should still win, but by a smaller margin.

Router note: a fixed permutation moves MNIST's mostly-blank border pixels to different coordinates per task, which gives a linear router a genuine, generalizing signal to identify which permutation produced a sample — reported below.
